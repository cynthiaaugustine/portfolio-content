My Design Principles

On this page you'll find a list of design principles that I follow.

I've curated this to provide guidance for designing ethically humane digital products through patterns focused on user-centred design.

1. Design with Empathy

In User Experience, the user's needs, goals and experiences should take precedence throughout the design process. Having empathy in design means understanding users: not just their needs, but also their constraints and the context of their situation as a whole.

2. Keep it Simple

Time and time again I've learned that simple designs win over the complex ones. Our goal as designers is to help users achieve their goals in the most straightforward possible way. Therefore, I believe that it's important to always design for the 'path of least resistance'. Less is more!

3. Make Mistakes Earlier in the Process

The quicker your learn from your design mistakes, the quicker you can start identifying areas of improvement. That's where user testing comes into play. Don't underestimat the power of testinig.
